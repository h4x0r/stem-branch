# Qi Men Dun Jia (奇門遁甲): The 拆補法 vs 置閏法 Controversy

## Research Notes for Academic Paper

---

## 1. What is Qi Men Dun Jia?

Qi Men Dun Jia (奇門遁甲, literally "Mysterious Gates Escaping Jia") is one of the Three Styles (三式, sānshì) of classical Chinese divination, alongside Da Liu Ren (大六壬) and Tai Yi Shen Shu (太乙神數). Originally developed for military strategy, it integrates principles from the Yijing, yinyang cosmology, the Five Phases (五行), Heavenly Stems and Earthly Branches, and astronomical observation.

### The Name Decoded

- **奇 (Qí)**: The three "extraordinary" Heavenly Stems — 乙 (Yǐ), 丙 (Bǐng), 丁 (Dīng) — which serve as the "three wonders" (三奇)
- **門 (Mén)**: The Eight Gates (八門), one of the system's analytical layers
- **遁甲 (Dùn Jiǎ)**: "Concealed Jiǎ" — the stem 甲 is the commander element, symbolically hidden among other stems during calculation

### The Six Key Components

#### 1. 九宮 (Jiǔ Gōng) — Nine Palaces
A 3×3 magic square (cosmic board / 式盤) corresponding to the eight directional sectors of the post-heaven bagua plus a central palace. Each palace has an associated element, direction, and trigram. The Nine Palaces form the spatial foundation upon which all other elements are overlaid.

#### 2. 八門 (Bā Mén) — Eight Gates (the "Human Plate" / 人盤)
The eight gates represent the human dimension of analysis:
- 休門 (Xiū) — Rest/Luck
- 生門 (Shēng) — Life/Growth
- 傷門 (Shāng) — Injury
- 杜門 (Dù) — Obstruction
- 景門 (Jǐng) — Scenery/Brilliance
- 死門 (Sǐ) — Death
- 驚門 (Jīng) — Startling/Dismay
- 開門 (Kāi) — Opening

The most auspicious gates are 休, 開, and 生; the others are inauspicious.

#### 3. 九星 (Jiǔ Xīng) — Nine Stars (the "Heaven Plate" / 天盤)
Celestial influences derived from the Big Dipper constellation:
天心, 天蓬, 天任, 天冲, 天辅, 天英, 天禽, 天芮, 天柱

Each star represents a quality of celestial energy affecting the reading.

#### 4. 八神 (Bā Shén) — Eight Spirits/Deities
Supernatural helpers considered "aid from heaven":
值符 (Zhí Fú, leader), 腾蛇 (Téng Shé), 太阴 (Tài Yīn), 六合 (Liù Hé), 白虎 (Bái Hǔ), 玄武 (Xuán Wǔ), 九地 (Jiǔ Dì), 九天 (Jiǔ Tiān)

#### 5. 天干 (Tiān Gān) — Ten Heavenly Stems
甲, 乙, 丙, 丁, 戊, 己, 庚, 辛, 壬, 癸

In QMDJ, 甲 is concealed under the six 儀 (instruments: 戊, 己, 庚, 辛, 壬, 癸), while 乙, 丙, 丁 serve as the three wonders.

#### 6. 地支 (Dì Zhī) — Twelve Earthly Branches
子, 丑, 寅, 卯, 辰, 巳, 午, 未, 申, 酉, 戌, 亥

Combined with the Heavenly Stems, they form the sexagenary (60-unit) cycle that is fundamental to timing calculations.

### What is a 局 (Jú)?

A 局 (formation/configuration) is the specific arrangement of all symbolic elements (stars, gates, spirits, stems) across the Nine Palaces for a given moment in time. Each 局 represents one of 1,080 distinct cosmic configurations that cycle through the year.

## 2. How Are 局 Assigned?

### 陽遁 (Yáng Dùn) and 陰遁 (Yīn Dùn)

The year is divided into two halves:
- **陽遁 (Yang Dun / Forward Sequence)**: From Winter Solstice (冬至) to Summer Solstice (夏至) — energy is ascending
- **陰遁 (Yin Dun / Reverse Sequence)**: From Summer Solstice (夏至) to Winter Solstice (冬至) — energy is descending

Each half contains 9 possible 局 numbers (1-9), yielding 18 base formations.

### 上元/中元/下元 (Upper/Middle/Lower Yuan)

Each of the 24 solar terms spans approximately 15 days and is divided into three 5-day periods:
- **上元 (Shàng Yuán)** — Upper Period
- **中元 (Zhōng Yuán)** — Middle Period
- **下元 (Xià Yuán)** — Lower Period

The 符頭 (Fú Tóu, "talisman head") system determines which yuan applies:
- **Upper Yuan**: 符頭 day has Earthly Branch 子, 午, 卯, or 酉 (four Cardinal branches)
- **Middle Yuan**: 符頭 day has Earthly Branch 寅, 申, 巳, or 亥 (four Growth branches)
- **Lower Yuan**: 符頭 day has Earthly Branch 辰, 戌, 丑, or 未 (four Storage branches)

The 符頭 is always a 甲 or 己 day (the start of each 5-day sub-cycle within the sexagenary system).

### The Ju Number Assignment

Each solar term is assigned three 局 numbers (one per yuan). The classical mnemonic verses (歌訣) encode these:

**Yang Dun verse**: 「冬至驚蟄一七四，小寒二八五同推。春分大寒三九六，立春八五二相隨。穀雨小滿五二八，雨水九六三為期。清明立夏四一七，芒種六三九為宜。」

**Yin Dun verse**: 「夏至白露九三六，小暑八二五之間，大暑秋分七一四，立秋二五八遁還，霜降小雪五八二，大雪四七一相關，處暑排來一四七，立冬寒露九六三（六九三）。」

### How Many 局 Are There?

The number depends on how you count:

- **4,320**: The "original" count — 360 days × 12 時辰 (double-hours) per day = 4,320 configurations per year. This is the legendary system attributed to the Yellow Emperor.
- **1,080**: The practical count — 4,320 ÷ 4 (each formation repeats 4 times per year) = 1,080 unique configurations. Attributed to Feng Hou (風后). This is the standard number cited in practice: 9 Yang Dun × 60 Jiazi + 9 Yin Dun × 60 Jiazi = 540 + 540 = 1,080.
- **72**: Solar-term based count — 24 solar terms × 3 yuan = 72. Attributed to Jiang Taigong (姜太公).
- **18**: The board count — 1,080 ÷ 60 = 18 rotating boards (9 Yang + 9 Yin). Attributed to Zhang Liang (張良).

These are not reductions but compressions; no information is lost.

## 3. The Core Problem: 360 vs 365.2422 Days

The QMDJ system maps 24 solar terms × 3 yuan × 5 days = 360 days per year. But Earth's orbital period is approximately 365.2422 days. The discrepancy of ~5.25 days per year creates a progressive misalignment between the sexagenary cycle's 符頭 markers and the actual astronomical solar term transitions.

This misalignment manifests as three possible states:

- **正授 (Zhèng Shòu)**: The 符頭 and solar term transition coincide exactly (rare)
- **超神 (Chāo Shén)**: The 符頭 arrives *before* the solar term (the symbolic cycle "runs ahead")
- **接氣 (Jiē Qì)**: The solar term arrives *before* the 符頭 (the symbolic cycle "lags behind")

How to handle this discrepancy is the central controversy.

## 4. 拆補法 (Chāi Bǔ Fǎ) — The Splitting and Supplementing Method

### Core Principle

「以節氣為體，以干支為用」 — Solar terms serve as the framework (substance); stem-branch cycles serve as the functional tool.

**Rule**: The moment a new solar term begins, switch immediately to that solar term's designated 局 numbers. No mixing of different solar terms' configurations is permitted.

### How the Split Works at a Solar Term Boundary

When a new solar term (節氣) arrives in the middle of a 5-day yuan cycle:

1. The remaining days of the current yuan under the *old* solar term are "split off" (拆)
2. The system immediately switches to the *new* solar term's appropriate yuan
3. The "leftover" days from the split are "supplemented" (補) at the end of the new solar term's cycle

This produces characteristic patterns:
- **殘上—中元—下元—補上**: If the split occurs during an upper yuan, the remnant upper period is followed by middle, lower, then the supplemented upper period completes the cycle
- **殘中—下元—上元—補中**: If split during middle yuan
- **殘下—上元—中元—補下**: If split during lower yuan

### Determining Which Yuan After the Split

Even after switching to the new solar term, the system still follows the sexagenary cycle rule:
- 子午卯酉 days = Upper Yuan (上元)
- 寅申巳亥 days = Middle Yuan (中元)
- 辰戌丑未 days = Lower Yuan (下元)

### Precision Requirements

The 拆補法 requires knowing the **exact time** of each solar term transition (to the hour at minimum, ideally to the minute). Since the method switches 局 at the precise moment of the solar term boundary, any error in calculating that moment directly affects which 局 is selected.

### Advocates and Texts

- **張志春 (Zhāng Zhìchūn)**: Major modern proponent; author of influential Qimen texts
- **Modern majority**: Most contemporary QMDJ practitioners and teachers now use 拆補法
- First recorded in the Song Dynasty (宋代) text 《遁甲符應經》(Dùnjiǎ Fúyìng Jīng), during the Jingyou era (景佑, 1034-1038 CE)
- The method gained widespread adoption from the Ming-Qing period onward as QMDJ spread from military to civilian use

### Arguments For

1. **Natural alignment**: Follows the Daoist principle 「人法地，地法天，天法道，道法自然」 — conforming to nature rather than imposing artificial adjustments
2. **Simplicity**: Much easier to learn and apply than the intercalary method
3. **Practical validation**: Proponents claim it produces accurate divination results
4. **No artificial stretching**: Does not require doubling any solar term's duration
5. **Accessibility**: Makes QMDJ learnable for ordinary students, not just palace scholars

## 5. 置閏法 (Zhì Rùn Fǎ) — The Intercalary Method

### Core Principle

Strictly follow the sexagenary (干支) cycle. Each 60-時辰 cycle must complete fully before transitioning. The accumulated surplus days are handled through periodic intercalation.

### How "Excess" Days Are Handled

Each solar term's three yuan total 15 days, but the actual solar term period averages ~15.2184 days. The excess accumulates over time:
- Per solar term: ~0.2184 days surplus
- Over 24 solar terms: ~5.25 days surplus
- This surplus manifests as the 超神 gap growing wider

### The Nine-Day Rule (超不過九，接不過五)

The critical threshold is codified as: "超神 must not exceed 9 days; 接氣 must not exceed 5 days."

When the gap between the 符頭 and the solar term exceeds 9 days, a **置閏** (intercalation) must be performed.

### What Constitutes a 閏奇 / 閏局?

A 閏奇 (intercalary Qi) or 閏局 (intercalary formation) is the repetition of a complete upper-middle-lower three-yuan cycle for a specific solar term. This effectively doubles that solar term's duration from 15 to 30 days, allowing the symbolic cycle to "catch up" with the astronomical reality.

### When Intercalation Occurs

Intercalation is placed at specific solar terms:
- **芒種 (Máng Zhǒng / Grain in Ear)**: Before the Summer Solstice
- **大雪 (Dà Xuě / Heavy Snow)**: Before the Winter Solstice

These are chosen because they immediately precede the two solstices, which mark the transition between Yang Dun and Yin Dun. The 符頭 must be realigned before each solstice.

### Relationship to Chinese Calendar Intercalation

The concept parallels the 閏月 (intercalary month) in the Chinese lunisolar calendar. Just as the calendar inserts an extra month approximately every 3 years to keep lunar months aligned with solar terms, the QMDJ system inserts an extra three-yuan cycle to keep 符頭 markers aligned with solar term boundaries. In the calendar, accumulated surplus days produce 閏月; in QMDJ, accumulated surplus 時辰 produce 閏奇.

### Practical Example

December 7, 1898 (甲辰日, 巳時): 大雪 solar term arrived. But the 符頭 甲午 had occurred on November 27 — already exceeding the solar term by 11 days. The intercalary procedure:
1. Nov 27: 大雪 Upper Yuan
2. Dec 2: 大雪 Middle Yuan
3. Dec 7: 大雪 Lower Yuan
4. Dec 12: 大雪 Upper Yuan (intercalary repeat)
5. Dec 17: 大雪 Middle Yuan (intercalary repeat)
6. Dec 22: 大雪 Lower Yuan (intercalary repeat)

After this 6-period sequence, the 超神 condition converts to 接氣 for the following term.

### Advocates and Texts

- **Classical orthodoxy**: The original/ancient method (古法)
- **《奇門遁甲統宗》(Qí Mén Dùn Jiǎ Tǒng Zōng)**: Major classical compendium that details the intercalary method
- **《煙波釣叟賦》(Yānbō Diàosǒu Fù)**: The canonical foundational verse of QMDJ tradition, which encodes 超神接氣 principles
- **《遁甲演義》(Dùn Jiǎ Yǎn Yì)**: Ming dynasty text by Cheng Daosheng (程道生) that discusses both methods
- **Qing dynasty scholar Jiang Yong (江永)**: Wrote about intercalary methods in 《河洛精蘊》(Hé Luò Jīng Yùn)
- Traditional military Qimen (運籌) practitioners

### Arguments For

1. **Historical authenticity**: The original method of the tradition
2. **Sexagenary integrity**: Preserves the completeness of the 60-unit Jiazi cycle
3. **Theoretical rigor**: Each cycle completes fully, maintaining the mathematical elegance of the system
4. **Military heritage**: Designed for the precision demanded by military applications

### Arguments Against

1. **Artificial doubling**: Extends certain solar terms from 15 to 30 days, which critics say violates astronomical reality
2. **Complexity**: Requires mastery of 超神, 正授, 接氣, and 置閏 concepts — historically a barrier to learning
3. **Human artifice**: The choice of when exactly to intercalate involves subjective judgment
4. **Accessibility**: Historically kept QMDJ as "the Emperor's Study" (帝王之學), inaccessible to common people

## 6. The Third Method: 茅山法 (Máo Shān Fǎ)

Also called 無閏法 (Wú Rùn Fǎ, "No-Intercalation Method").

Created by Maoshan Daoist monks during the Yuan-Ming dynasties (元明時期, roughly 13th-16th century).

**Mechanism**: From the exact moment of the solar term transition, use the Upper Yuan 局. After 60 時辰 (5 days), switch to Middle Yuan. After another 60 時辰, switch to Lower Yuan. If the previous solar term's cycle hasn't finished when the new one arrives, it is simply cut off.

**Advantage**: Does not disrupt the upper-middle-lower sequence; does not require intercalation.

**Disadvantage**: Does not follow the 子午卯酉/寅申巳亥/辰戌丑未 system for determining yuan periods.

## 7. The Debate: Summary of Arguments

### For 拆補法 (Split-and-Supplement)

| Argument | Detail |
|---|---|
| Natural alignment | Follows actual astronomical solar term boundaries |
| Simplicity | Easy to learn, easy to apply |
| Daoist philosophy | 「道法自然」— the Dao follows Nature |
| Modern validation | Widely tested by contemporary practitioners |
| Accessibility | Democratized QMDJ beyond elite scholars |

### For 置閏法 (Intercalary)

| Argument | Detail |
|---|---|
| Historical orthodoxy | The original ancient method |
| Cycle integrity | Preserves the complete 60-unit Jiazi cycle |
| Classical authority | Encoded in the 煙波釣叟賦 |
| Military precision | Designed for the rigor of military strategy |
| Theoretical completeness | A self-consistent mathematical framework |

### Is There a "Correct" Answer?

There is no definitive consensus. The debate has persisted for nearly a millennium (since at least the Song dynasty). Key considerations:

1. **Empirical testing is difficult**: Divination outcomes are inherently hard to validate systematically
2. **The methods diverge only near solar term boundaries**: For most of the year, the methods produce identical results. Differences arise only in the "transition zone" around solar term changes
3. **Different applications may favor different methods**: Military strategy (the original use case) may benefit from the rigor of 置閏法, while daily divination may benefit from the accessibility of 拆補法
4. **Modern trend**: The majority of contemporary practitioners have adopted 拆補法, though some traditionalists maintain that the 置閏法 is the only authentic approach

### When Did the Split Between Methods Occur?

The earliest documented coexistence of both methods is in the **Song dynasty** (specifically the Jingyou era, 1034-1038 CE), in the 《遁甲符應經》. However, the 置閏法 is the older "ancient method" (古法), meaning the split likely occurred when the 拆補法 was first proposed as an alternative — possibly as early as the late Tang or early Song period.

## 9. Computational Precision Requirements

### How Sensitive Is 局 Assignment to Solar Term Timing Errors?

The sensitivity depends on which method is used:

#### Under 拆補法 (highest sensitivity to solar term timing)

Since the 拆補法 switches 局 at the **exact moment** of solar term transition, timing precision is critical. The "danger zone" exists whenever a divination is performed near a solar term boundary.

**Quantifying the danger zone:**

- A 時辰 (double-hour) lasts 2 hours
- If a solar term transition falls within the same 時辰 as the divination moment, the 局 number will differ between the old and new solar term
- Near the transition, being off by even minutes can yield a completely different chart
- The danger zone is approximately **±2 hours** around the solar term transition time

**Required precision:**
- For modern practice: Solar term times should be calculated to at least **minute-level precision**
- For computational implementations: Second-level precision is achievable and recommended

#### Under 置閏法 (lower sensitivity to solar term timing)

The intercalary method is less sensitive to exact solar term timing because:
1. It relies primarily on the sexagenary cycle (which is deterministic and exact)
2. Solar term timing only matters for determining *when* to intercalate
3. The 9-day threshold provides a larger buffer

However, timing still matters for determining the 超神 gap accurately.

#### Under 茅山法 (similar sensitivity to 拆補法)

Uses the exact solar term moment as the starting point for the upper yuan, so timing precision requirements are comparable to 拆補法.

### Computational Methods for Solar Term Times

Modern computation of solar term times uses astronomical algorithms:

1. **VSOP87 theory** (Bretagnon & Francou, 1987): The standard planetary theory. Full VSOP87 achieves accuracy better than 0.01 arcsecond in ecliptic longitude, corresponding to **sub-second timing precision** for solar term moments.

2. **Jean Meeus's Astronomical Algorithms** (truncated VSOP87): Achieves accuracy within a few arcseconds, corresponding to timing precision on the order of **seconds** for solar term moments.

3. **NREL Solar Position Algorithm (SPA)**: Based on VSOP87, achieves ±0.0003° uncertainty from -2000 to 6000 CE.

4. **Historical limitation — ΔT**: For historical dates, the main source of uncertainty is ΔT (the difference between Terrestrial Time and Universal Time). For dates far from the present, ΔT uncertainty can reach **minutes to hours**, making precise historical chart reconstruction increasingly uncertain.

### Two Definitions of Solar Terms

An additional source of computational divergence:

- **平氣法 (Píng Qì Fǎ / Equal Term Method)**: Divides the tropical year into 24 equal time intervals. This was the historical method.
- **定氣法 (Dìng Qì Fǎ / True Term Method)**: Defines solar terms by the Sun's ecliptic longitude (every 15°). This was introduced by the Shixian calendar (時憲曆) in 1645 and is the modern standard.

These two methods can produce slightly different transition times, adding another dimension to the controversy. Traditional QMDJ texts used 平氣法, but modern astronomical computation uses 定氣法.

### Practical Impact Assessment

For the vast majority of divination moments (i.e., times not near a solar term boundary), all three methods produce identical results. Differences arise only:
- Within ~1-2 days of a solar term transition (for 拆補法 vs 置閏法)
- Every 2-3 years when intercalation would be triggered (for 置閏法 specifically)
- At the exact hour of transition (for minute-level sensitivity)

The probability that a random divination moment falls in the "danger zone" is approximately: 2 days / 15 days per solar term ≈ 13% of the time. However, the *impact* of the difference is that an entirely different 局 number would be selected, changing all star, gate, spirit, and stem arrangements — producing a fundamentally different reading.

## 10. Summary for Academic Paper

The 拆補法 vs 置閏法 debate is fundamentally a question of **what constitutes the primary temporal framework** in Qi Men Dun Jia:

- **置閏法** privileges the sexagenary cycle as primary, adjusting for astronomical reality through periodic intercalation
- **拆補法** privileges astronomical solar terms as primary, adjusting the sexagenary cycle by splitting and supplementing yuan periods
- **茅山法** also privileges solar terms but abandons the branch-based yuan determination system entirely

The controversy has persisted since at least the Song dynasty (11th century) and shows no sign of resolution. It reflects a deeper tension in Chinese cosmological thought between mathematical regularity (the sexagenary cycle) and observational astronomy (solar term transitions).

For computational implementations, the choice of method has direct implications:
- 拆補法 requires precise solar term calculations (to the minute or second)
- 置閏法 requires tracking the 超神 gap and implementing intercalary logic
- Both require accurate sexagenary cycle computation

Modern software typically implements 拆補法 due to its simplicity and the availability of precise astronomical algorithms (VSOP87) that eliminate historical uncertainties in solar term timing.
