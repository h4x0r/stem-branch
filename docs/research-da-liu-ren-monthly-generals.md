# Da Liu Ren (大六壬) and the Twelve Monthly Generals (十二月將)

## Academic Research Notes — Compiled 2026-03-22

---

## 1. Overview of Da Liu Ren (大六壬)

Da Liu Ren (大六壬) is an ancient Chinese divination system dating from the later Warring States period (c. 475–221 BCE). It is one of the **Three Styles** (三式 sānshì) of Chinese elite divination, alongside **Qi Men Dun Jia** (奇門遁甲) and **Tai Yi Shen Shu** (太乙神數).

### Etymology

The name "Da Liu Ren" (Great Six Ren) comes from the six occurrences of the heavenly stem 壬 (rén, "yang water") in the Sexagenary Cycle. The philosophical basis draws from the Hetu (河圖): "天一生水，地六成之" — "Heavenly One creates water, Earthly Six produces it."

### Core Components

| Component | Chinese | Description |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Earth Plate** | 地盤 dìpán | Fixed plate carrying the twelve Earthly Branches |
| **Heaven Plate** | 天盤 tiānpán | Rotating plate placed over the Earth Plate; models celestial motion |
| **Four Classes** | 四課 sìkè | Four prognostications derived from the day's stem and branch |
| **Three Transmissions** | 三傳 sānchuán | Initial (初傳), Middle (中傳), and Final (末傳) transmissions derived from the Four Classes |

There are **720 possible combinations** of classes and transmissions.

### Chart Casting Process

1. Determine the **Monthly General** (月將) based on the Sun's zodiacal position
2. Place the Monthly General on the hour branch to form the Heaven Plate
3. Derive the Four Classes from the day's stem and branch
4. Calculate the Three Transmissions from the Four Classes
5. Note vacancies (空亡 kōngwáng) and other factors

## 2. The Twelve Monthly Generals (十二月將)

The 月將 (Monthly General) represents the commander-spirit of a month. It is determined by the Sun's position in the twelve zodiacal mansions (黃道十二宮). The alternative name for 月將 in Liu Ren is simply **太陽** (the Sun).

### The Six-Harmony (六合) Principle

The 月將 is the six-harmony (六合) counterpart of the 月建 (monthly establishment):

- 月建 runs forward: 寅→卯→辰→巳→午→未→申→酉→戌→亥→子→丑
- 月將 runs backward: 亥→戌→酉→申→未→午→巳→辰→卯→寅→丑→子

This reflects the six-harmony pairings: 寅↔亥, 卯↔戌, 辰↔酉, 巳↔申, 午↔未, 子↔丑.

## 3. Is 月將 Historically 月相?

### Short Answer: No

The terms 月將 and 月相 are **distinct concepts** that were never used interchangeably:

- **月將 (yuè jiàng)**: "Monthly General/Commander" — a divinatory concept specific to Liu Ren
- **月相 (yuè xiàng)**: "Lunar phases" — an astronomical/calendrical concept (new moon, first quarter, full moon, etc.)

### The Indirect Historical Connection

The two concepts are historically linked through the **合朔** (héshuò, lunisolar conjunction / new moon). The《五行大義》explains the six-harmony principle as "日月行次之合" (the conjunction of the paths of the Sun and Moon). On the first day of each lunar month (朔日, new moon), the Sun and Moon occupy the same zodiacal mansion, and this conjunction point originally defined the Monthly General.

### Historical Alternative Terms for 月將

Classical sources use these alternative terms — none of which is 月相:

| Term | Meaning | Usage |
|------|---------|-------|
| 合神 héshen | Conjunction Spirit | Original pre-precession term |
| 太陽 tàiyáng | The Sun | Emphasizes solar nature |
| 太陽過宮 tàiyáng guò gōng | Sun transiting mansions | Astronomical description |
| 日躔 rìchán | Solar transit/station | Song Dynasty technical term |

## 4. How Does 月將 Change? The 中氣 Question

### Three Historical Methods

#### Method 1: 合神法 (Conjunction Spirit Method) — OLDEST

Changes at each lunar month boundary (new moon). Uses the six-harmony counterpart of the monthly establishment. This was the original method.

#### Method 2: 日躔法 (Solar Transit Method) — SONG DYNASTY REFORM

Changes when the Sun enters a new sidereal star lodge. Established by Yang Weide (楊惟德) during the Northern Song (c. 1034–1038 CE) in the《景祐六壬神定經》. Shen Kuo (沈括) advocated this method in《夢溪筆談》.

#### Method 3: 中氣換將法 — MODERN SIMPLIFICATION

Changes at each 中氣 (principal solar term). This is a post-Song simplification of the 日躔 method, adopted when the astronomical knowledge behind precise solar transit calculations was lost.

### Why They Originally Coincided

In the ancient quarter-remainder calendar (古四分曆) era, the Big Dipper's handle pointed toward 析木 when the monthly establishment was 寅, and on the first day of the first lunar month, the Sun and Moon conjoined at the 娵訾 star lodge (亥 palace). Thus 合神, 太陽過宮, and 中氣 all indicated the same result.

### The Precession Problem

Due to axial precession (~50.27″/year, or 1° every 71.6 years), these three methods diverged over centuries. Shen Kuo noted in《夢溪筆談》(c. 1088) that by the Song Dynasty the divergence was already about half a mansion (~15°). By 2026, the accumulated precession since the Han Dynasty is approximately **28°** — nearly one full mansion.

### Shen Kuo's Argument

From《夢溪筆談》卷七 "象術一":

> 亥曰登明，為正月將；戌曰天魁，為二月將。古人謂之合神，又謂之太陽過宮。

Shen Kuo argued that if one does not use the Sun's actual position (太陽躔次), then "日月、五星、二十八宿，皆不應天行" — the Sun, Moon, five planets, and 28 lunar mansions would all fail to correspond to actual celestial movements.

## 5. Solar and Lunar Dependencies

### Solar Dependencies (PRIMARY)

| Component | Solar Dependency | Precision Needed |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Monthly General (月將) | Sun's ecliptic longitude | ~1° (for switching day) |
| Solar Terms (節氣/中氣) | Sun reaching multiples of 15° ecliptic longitude | ~0.1° (~6 hours) for boundary days |
| Heaven Plate (天盤) | Models the Sun's annual motion | Indirect (via 月將) |

The Heaven Plate "代表地球圍繞太陽公轉的位置" (represents Earth's orbital position around the Sun).

### Lunar Dependencies (SECONDARY)

| Component | Lunar Dependency | Precision Needed |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Lunar calendar month | Synodic month (new moon cycle) | ~few hours (for 合朔 timing) |
| Historical 合神 method | New moon timing determines 月將 switch | ~few hours |
| Modern 中氣 method | **No lunar dependency** | N/A |

### Key Insight

The modern 中氣 method requires **only solar computation** — no lunar computation at all. The historical 合神 method required **both solar and lunar computation** (since the new moon depends on both the Sun's and Moon's positions). The 日躔 method required **precise solar computation with precession correction**.

| Period | Development |

## 7. The 中氣 Rule: Original vs. Modern

### Summary

| Aspect | Original Method | Song Reform | Modern Practice |
|--------|----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Name** | 合神法 | 日躔法 | 中氣換將法 |
| **Trigger** | New moon (朔日) | Sun enters new star lodge | 中氣 solar term |
| **Reference frame** | Lunar month | Sidereal zodiac | Tropical zodiac |
| **Solar dependency** | Indirect | Direct (precise) | Direct (approximate) |
| **Lunar dependency** | Yes | No | No |
| **Precession-aware** | Was accurate when created | Yes (recalculated) | No (fixed to tropical frame) |
| **Status today** | Minority practice | Lost knowledge | Mainstream practice |

### The Fundamental Issue

The 中氣 method uses the **tropical** zodiac (fixed to the equinoxes), while the original star lodges (星次) were **sidereal** (fixed to the stars). Due to precession, these frames diverge by ~1° every 72 years. The current divergence (~28°) means that the modern 中氣 method places the Sun in a different star lodge than the original system would — a discrepancy of nearly one full mansion.
