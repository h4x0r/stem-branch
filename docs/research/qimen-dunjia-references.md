Compiled 2026-03-26. All citations verified via web search; no fabricated references.

## 1. Historical Origins and Legendary Attributions

### Ho Peng Yoke (2003) -- The Three Cosmic Boards

- **Citation:** Ho Peng Yoke. *Chinese Mathematical Astrology: Reaching Out to the Stars*. London: Routledge (Needham Research Institute Series), 2003.
- **DOI:** [10.4324/9780203633632](https://doi.org/10.4324/9780203633632)
- **ISBN:** 978-0-415-29759-2 (hardcover), 978-0-415-86310-0 (paperback)
- **Chapter 4 DOI:** [10.4324/9780203633632-4](https://doi.org/10.4324/9780203633632-4) ("Qimen Dunjia: Strange Gates Escaping Techniques")
- **Taylor & Francis:** <https://www.taylorfrancis.com/books/mono/10.4324/9780203633632/chinese-mathematical-astrology-ho-peng-yoke>
- **Google Books:** <https://books.google.com/books/about/Chinese_Mathematical_Astrology.html?id=FHBCyHgKkMwC>
- **Archive.org (full text):** <https://archive.org/details/chinesemathematicalastrologyhopengyokeroutledge_202003_54_P>
- **Note:** First comprehensive English-language treatment of the Three Cosmic Boards (三式 sanshi): Taiyi, Qimen Dunjia, and Liuren. Describes legendary attributions (黃帝, 九天玄女), computational algorithms, nine-palace layout, 1,080 chart system (540 Yang + 540 Yin), and historical development. Translates 奇門遁甲 as "Strange Gates Escaping Techniques." Reviewed in *EASTM* 23 (2005): <https://files01.core.ac.uk/download/pdf/228877327.pdf>.

### Kalinowski (2009) -- Diviners and Astrologers under the Eastern Zhou

- **Citation:** Marc Kalinowski. "Diviners and Astrologers under the Eastern Zhou: Transmitted Texts and Recent Archaeological Discoveries." In John Lagerwey and Marc Kalinowski (eds.), *Early Chinese Religion: Part One: Shang through Han (1250 BC--220 AD)*. Leiden: Brill, 2009. Pp. 341--396.
- **ISBN:** 978-90-04-16835-0; e-ISBN: 978-90-47-44242-4
- **Brill:** <https://brill.com/display/book/edcoll/9789047442424/Bej.9789004168350.i-1312_010.xml>
- **Note:** Examines archaeological discoveries of mantic instruments and transmitted textual evidence for divination practices from Warring States through Han.

### Loewe (1994) -- Divination, Mythology and Monarchy

- **Citation:** Michael Loewe. *Divination, Mythology and Monarchy in Han China*. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press (University of Cambridge Oriental Publications 48), 1994.
- **ISBN:** 978-0-521-45466-7 (hardcover), 978-0-521-05220-7 (paperback)
- **Google Books:** <https://books.google.com/books/about/Divination_Mythology_and_Monarchy_in_Han.html?id=7hApAtRK3-AC>
- **Note:** Links between religion and statecraft in Han dynasty (206 BC--AD 220). Discusses divination techniques used by emperors to forecast the future. Broader cosmological context underlying dunjia-era practices.

## 2. Key Classical Texts

### Cheng Daosheng -- *Dunjia yanyi* 遁甲演義

- **Citation:** Cheng Daosheng 程道生. *Dunjia yanyi* 遁甲演義. 4 juan. Late Ming dynasty (1368--1644). Included in *Siku quanshu* 四庫全書.
- **ChinaKnowledge.de:** <http://www.chinaknowledge.de/Literature/Daoists/dunjiayanyi.html>
- **Chinese Text Project (partial):** <https://ctext.org/wiki.pl?if=en&res=611519>
- **Note:** Comprehensive compendium of the dunjia method. Quotes from *Huangdi Yinfujing* 黃帝陰符經. Explains nine-palace grid (九宮), three oddities (三奇 = 乙丙丁), six instruments (六儀), eight gates (八門), nine stars (九星). Submitted to the *Siku Quanshu* compilation by Yao Youruo 姚有若. Challenges conflation of Hetu/Luoshu interpretations.

### *Yanbo Diaosou Ge* 煙波釣叟歌

- **Context:** Discussed in Ho Peng Yoke (2003), Chapter 4.
- **Note:** Verse text encoding Qimen Dunjia principles. Bears similarities to the shorter *Huangdi Yinfujing*. Contains the legendary origin story: Yellow Emperor vs Chi You, Feng Hou recording the arts. Identical cosmological verses about Yin/Yang ascending/descending orders and Nine Palaces magic square.

### *Dunjia Wanyi Jue* 遁甲萬一訣

- **Chinese Text Project:** <https://ctext.org/datawiki.pl?if=en&res=853309>
- **Note:** Single-volume text attributed to Tang-dynasty Li Jing, compiling from Yellow Emperor's teachings. Covers the eight gates (休生傷杜景死驚開) for prognostication of national fortunes.

## 3. The Computational Algorithm

### Ho Peng Yoke (2003), Chapter 4

- (See Section 1 above for full citation)
- **Covers:** 局數 determination (陽遁 yang ascending / 陰遁 yin descending), 三奇六儀 (three wonders and six instruments) layout, 八門 (eight gates), 九星 (nine stars), 九宮 (nine palaces) structure, the 1,080 chart system. Describes 時家奇門 (hourly Qimen) computational procedures.

### Leong (2024) -- SSRN Paper

- **Citation:** David Leong. "Interdisciplinary Approaches to 奇门遁甲 Qimen Dunjia for Decision Making: Complexity, Quantum Theory, and Predictive Science." SSRN, 2024. 37 pages.
- **DOI:** [10.2139/ssrn.4995004](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.4995004)
- **SSRN:** <https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4995004>
- **Note:** Describes QM structural elements: Heavenly Stems (天干), Earthly Branches (地支), Nine Stars (九星), Eight Deities/Spirits (八神), Eight Trigrams (八卦), Eight Doors (八門) mapped into 3x3 matrix (九宮格). Applies complexity science and quantum theory frameworks to QMDJ decision-making.

### Lo (2017) -- Modern Qi Men Dun Jia

- **Citation:** Raymond Lo. "Modern Qi Men Dun Jia." *Cultural and Religious Studies* 5, no. 8 (August 2017): 515--523.
- **DOI:** [10.17265/2328-2177/2017.08.007](https://doi.org/10.17265/2328-2177/2017.08.007)
- **PDF:** <https://www.davidpublisher.com/Public/uploads/Contribute/5a7974a24f50b.pdf>
- **Note:** Describes two schools of Qi Men: Yang school (24 seasonal divisions) and Daoist/Yin school (mixed solar-lunar calendar). Modern applications for timing and situational analysis. Practical examples of chart construction.

### Dollhausen (2015) -- PhD Dissertation

- **Citation:** Nick Dollhausen. *Das chinesische Prognosesystem Qimen Dunjia -- 奇門遁甲 und der Wahrheitsgehalt seiner Aussagen hinsichtlich heilkundlicher/medizinischer Fragestellungen*. PhD dissertation, Europa-Universitat Viadrina Frankfurt (Oder), December 2015.
- **Repository:** <https://opus4.kobv.de/opus4-euv/frontdoor/index/index/year/2016/docId/211>
- **Note:** Systematic description of QMDJ as numerological prognostication system. Tests validity of health/disease predictions against actual outcomes. In German.

## 4. The Three Cosmic Boards (三式 Sanshi)

### Kalinowski (2022) -- Typology and Classification of the Mantic Arts

- **Citation:** Marc Kalinowski. "Typology and Classification of the Mantic Arts in China." In Michael Lackner and Zhao Lu (eds.), *Handbook of Divination and Prognostication in China*. Leiden: Brill (Handbook of Oriental Studies, Section 4 China, vol. 37), 2022. Pp. 174--251.
- **DOI:** [10.1163/9789004514263_006](https://doi.org/10.1163/9789004514263_006)
- **Brill:** <https://brill.com/display/book/edcoll/9789004514263/BP000013.xml>
- **ISBN:** 978-90-04-51127-9 (print); e-ISBN: 978-90-04-51426-3
- **Note:** Comprehensive typology of Chinese mantic arts. Classification evolution from pre-Han through later bibliographic catalogues. Contextualizes 三式 (sanshi) within the broader taxonomy of Chinese divination.

### Smith (2012) -- Overview of Divination in China

- **Citation:** Richard J. Smith. "An Overview of Divination in China from the Song through the Qing: Some Issues and Approaches." Paper presented at Workshop on Divinatory Traditions in East Asia, Rice University, February 17--18, 2012.
- **Academia.edu:** <https://www.academia.edu/29741910/>
- **ResearchGate PDF:** <https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Richard_Smith28/publication/312089869>
- **Note:** Survey of divination from Song through Qing. Discusses the three styles (三式 sanshi), state control via Qintianjian (欽天監 Imperial Astronomical Bureau), and classification of imperial-era mantic texts.

### Smith (1991) -- Fortune-tellers and Philosophers

- **Citation:** Richard J. Smith. *Fortune-tellers and Philosophers: Divination in Traditional Chinese Society*. Boulder, CO: Westview Press, 1991. Revised paperback 1993. Reprinted by Routledge, 2023. 446 pp.
- **DOI:** [10.4324/9780429039799](https://doi.org/10.4324/9780429039799)
- **ISBN:** 978-0-367-15980-1 (Routledge 2023); 978-0-813-37753-7 (Westview)
- **Note:** Comprehensive survey of all Chinese mantic techniques (Yijing, geomancy, astrology, numerology, physiognomy, dream interpretation). Discusses symbolism, aesthetics, ritual aspects, and social significance.

## 5. Modern Scholarly Analysis

### Kalinowski (2012--13) -- The Notion of "Shi" 式

- **Citation:** Marc Kalinowski. "The Notion of 'Shi' 式 and Some Related Terms in Qin-Han Calendrical Astrology." *Early China* 35--36 (2012--2013): 331--360.
- **Cambridge Core:** <https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/early-china/article/abs/notion-of-shi-and-some-related-terms-in-qinhan-calendrical-astrology/9902CD3C342AB764ACA286971C80B09C>
- **Note:** Etymology and semantics of 式 (shi). Argues the term cannot refer to a singular device. Discusses Fuyang 1977 archaeological finds. Proposes shizhan 式占 ("calendrical astrology") as translation framework.

### Kalinowski (1983) -- Les instruments astro-calenderiques des Han

- **Citation:** Marc Kalinowski. "Les instruments astro-calenderiques des Han et la methode Liu Ren." *Bulletin de l'Ecole francaise d'Extreme-Orient* 72 (1983): 309--419.
- **DOI:** [10.3406/befeo.1983.1463](https://doi.org/10.3406/befeo.1983.1463)
- **Persee (full text):** <https://www.persee.fr/doc/befeo_0336-1519_1983_num_72_1_1463>
- **Note:** Foundational survey of six known astro-calendrical instruments from the Han period. Bibliography of previous studies. Established modern academic framework for studying shi 式 cosmic boards and the Liuren mantic method.

### CiNii -- Basic Study of Qimen Dunjia

- **Citation:** "Basic Study for One of Chinese Divination; Qi men dun jia (奇門遁甲)." CiNii Research, CRID: 1050282813983856000.
- **URL:** <https://cir.nii.ac.jp/crid/1050282813983856000>
- **Note:** Japanese academic study decoding Yamakawa Kuichiro's *Qimen Dunjia Senkinshyo* (奇門遁甲千金書). Traces QMDJ references in *Siku Quanshu Zongmu*. Records transmission of 遁甲 to Japan per *Nihon Shoki* (602 CE, 10th year of Empress Suiko).

## 7. Archaeological Evidence

### Harper (1978) -- The Han Cosmic Board (Shih)

- **Citation:** Donald J. Harper. "The Han Cosmic Board (Shih)." *Early China* 4 (1978): 1--10.
- **Cambridge Core:** <https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/early-china/article/abs/han-cosmic-board-shih/2F2DFDBE0194DAA0FB4D16E9A06812B7>
- **Note:** Foundational study of 式盤 (shi pan) archaeological specimens. Bronze cosmic board described by Yen Tun-chieh. Fuyang (Anhui) tomb finds dated to 165 BC. Defines function as mechanistic model duplicating its macrocosmic counterpart.

### Harper (1981) -- Response to Cullen

- **Citation:** Donald J. Harper. "The Han Cosmic Board: A Response to Christopher Cullen." *Early China* 6 (1981): 47--56.
- **DOI:** [10.1017/S0362502800007550](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0362502800007550)
- **Cambridge Core:** <https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/early-china/article/abs/han-cosmic-board-a-response-to-christopher-cullen/253CE3215C44A6E4083440911F5D9D94>
- **Note:** Additional evidence for cosmic board shi. Relationship between Big Dipper on cosmic board and calendrical theory. Cosmic board divination texts in Daozang (道藏). Taoist absorption of cosmic board traditions.

### Kalinowski (2003) -- Dunhuang Manuscripts

- **Citation:** Marc Kalinowski (ed.). *Divination et societe dans la Chine medievale: Etude des manuscrits de Dunhuang de la Bibliotheque nationale de France et de la British Library*. Paris: Bibliotheque nationale de France, 2003. 669 pp.
- **ISBN:** 978-2-7177-2259-8
- **BnF page:** <https://editions.bnf.fr/divination-et-soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9-dans-la-chine-m%C3%A9di%C3%A9vale>
- **Persee review:** <https://www.persee.fr/doc/asie_0766-1177_2004_num_14_1_1216>
- **Note:** Studies mantic literature from Dunhuang Cave 17 (manuscripts from BnF Pelliot chinois collection and British Library). Chapters on hemerology, cleromancy, topomancy. Nine palaces, eight trigrams, sexagesimal cycle frameworks.

### Harper & Kalinowski (2017) -- Books of Fate

- **Citation:** Donald Harper and Marc Kalinowski (eds.). *Books of Fate and Popular Culture in Early China: The Daybook Manuscripts of the Warring States, Qin, and Han*. Leiden: Brill (Handbook of Oriental Studies, Section 4 China, vol. 33), 2017.
- **ISBN:** 978-90-04-31019-3
- **Brill:** <https://brill.com/view/title/23783>
- **Note:** Daybook manuscripts found in Warring States, Qin, and Han tombs (453 BCE--220 CE). Hemerology ("knowledge of good and bad days") in daily life. 11 contributors including Kalinowski, Cullen, Li Ling, Liu Lexian.

### Pelliot chinois 2615 -- Dunhuang Manuscript

- **Gallica (digitized):** <https://gallica.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/btv1b8307395h>
- **Note:** Digitized Dunhuang manuscript from the BnF Pelliot chinois collection. For scholarly context on dunjia-related Dunhuang manuscripts, see Kalinowski (2003).

### Kalinowski (1982) -- TLV Mirrors

- **Citation:** Marc Kalinowski. "Remarques sur les miroirs TLV dans leur rapport avec les tables de divination Liuren." *Numen* 29, no. 1 (1982): 114--120.
- **Note:** Relationship between TLV bronze mirrors and Liuren divination boards (六壬式). Material culture evidence for shi-type instruments.
