# Research: Xieji Bianfang Shu, Sanming Tonghui, Shen Sha Critique, and Shared Chart Problem

Date: 2026-03-23

## 1. Xieji Bianfang Shu (欽定協紀辨方書)

### Identity
- **Full title**: (Qinding) Xieji Bianfang Shu (欽定)協紀辨方書
- **English**: "Imperially Endorsed Treatise on Harmonizing Times and Distinguishing Directions"
- **Completed**: 1739 (not 1741 as sometimes cited)
- **Commissioner**: Qianlong Emperor 乾隆帝 (r. 1736--1795), who personally authored the preface
- **Length**: 36 juan, eleven parts
- **Included in**: Siku Quanshu 四庫全書

### Compilers
- **Primary supervisor**: Prince Yunlu 允祿 (1695--1767)
- **Co-compilers**: Mei Juecheng 梅瑴成 (1681--1763) and He Guozong 何國宗 (d. 1766), among others
- Penn Libraries catalog: ``Yunlu, Mei Juecheng, He Guozong deng zhuan'' (允祿, 梅瑴成, 何國宗等撰)
- Mei Juecheng was a court mathematician/astronomer, grandson of Mei Wending 梅文鼎

### Mythbuster Role
The text systematically evaluated and purged fabricated calendar spirits (shensha 神煞). Direct quote:

> "Specialists in arts loved the unusual and often engaged in fabrications. When they failed to invent [new calendar spirits], they fabricated different names for old calendar spirits, which were further altered and modified to cover up the tawdriness. Consequently, calendar spirits have proliferated... Now fabrications, once verified, will be deleted."

Two specific problems identified:
1. **同術異名** ("Different names for the same art"): several calendar spirits share a unified operative mechanism but have different names
2. **異術同名** ("Same name for different arts"): several calendar spirits share names but have different mechanisms

The Qingchao Wenxian Tongkao (清朝文獻考) states the reason for composition was that "in selecting auspicious days, the consideration in and before the three dynasties was only about the hardness and softness of the stems and branches, hardly about limitation and avoidance" -- the proliferation of restrictions was a later corruption.

### English Translation
Aylward, Thomas F. *The Imperial Guide to Feng Shui & Chinese Astrology: The Only Authentic Translation from the Original Chinese*. London: Watkins, 2007. 320 pp. ISBN 978-1842931752. (Partial translation)

## 2. Sanming Tonghui (三命通會)

### Identity
- **Title**: San Ming Tong Hui 三命通會 ("Comprehensive Discussions on the Three Destinies")
- **Author**: Wan Minying 萬民英, courtesy name Ruhao 汝豪, art name Yuwu 育吾
- **Dates**: 1521 or 1523--1603 (birth year disputed; Kotyk 2019 uses 1521)
- **Completed**: c. 1578
- **Length**: 12 juan, 600,000+ characters
- **Included in**: Siku Quanshu 四庫全書

### Author's Career
- From Daning 大寧 (modern Pingquan 平泉, Hebei)
- Jinshi degree holder (Jiajing 29th year, c. 1550)
- Investigating censor of Henan route; Right assistant administration commissioner of Fujian
- Fought Japanese pirates at Quanzhou
- Other works: Xingxue Dacheng 星學大成, Xinzi Xinjing 相字心經, Yijing Huijie 易經會解

### Comprehensiveness and the "Big Data" Question
The SMTH compiled BaZi analyses from 30+ masters across 200+ years of practice. It preserved:
- Ancient Lu Ming methods (pre-Zi Ping)
- Na Yin (Melodic Elements) system
- Extensive Shen Sha catalogues
- Newer Zi Ping Ten Gods system

**Assessment**: Describing it as "big data" is **anachronistic but defensible as analogy**. It systematically compiled thousands of example charts with life outcomes from 30+ masters. It explicitly "extracts essence, discards cumbersome and unfounded theories." However, it lacks randomization, controls, or statistical methods. Better framings: "proto-empirical large-scale case compilation" or "systematic encyclopedic synthesis."

### Competing Systems
The SMTH stands at the crossroads of:
1. **WuXing 五星 (Five Stars)** -- older, Year-pillar-based, uses NaYin 納音
2. **ZiPing 子平** -- newer, Day-pillar-based, uses Orthodox Five Elements (正五行 Zheng Wuxing)
3. **Shen Sha 神煞** -- symbolic stars catalogue

SMTH "regards the Year as the Base and Day as the Master" -- a transitional position. Later texts like Zi Ping Zhen Quan and Di Tian Sui "teach only theories from ZiPing and ignore completely theories from WuXing."

### Zheng Wuxing vs NaYin
- **Zheng Wuxing 正五行**: Examines each component's elemental nature individually (Jia = Wood, Geng = Metal)
- **NaYin 納音**: Examines the combined energy of stem-branch pairs via musical pitch theory (e.g., Wu Chen 戊辰 = both Earth individually, but "Great Forest Wood" in NaYin)
- NaYin derives from the Sixty Lu (ancient Chinese music theory pitches)
- SMTH preserved both; modern practice overwhelmingly uses Zheng Wuxing

## 3. Shen Sha Sampling Bias and Internal Critique Tradition

### The Shen Sha Debate
- Shen 神 = beneficial influence; Sha 煞 = baleful influence
- Not actual celestial bodies -- "energetic imprints" from Stem-Branch combinations
- Historical origin: ancient star worship, early calendar systems, Tang dynasty fate methods
- Original basis was astronomical (asterisms/fixed stars), but the framework shifted to 24 solar terms, creating a disconnect

### Internal Critical Evaluation Tradition
Key texts that critique or downplay Shen Sha within the tradition itself:
1. **Xieji Bianfang Shu** (1739): Imperial project explicitly deleting "fabricated" calendar spirits
2. **Di Tian Sui 滴天髓** with Ren Tieqiao 任鐵橋 (1773--1848) commentary: Emphasizes structural analysis (Day Master, Ten Gods) over Shen Sha. "Without superstition."
3. **Zi Ping Zhen Quan 子平真詮** (Shen Xiaozhan 沈孝瞻, Qing): "teaches only ZiPing theories, ignores WuXing theories completely"

### The Methodological Distinction
- **Empirically-grounded core**: Five Elements dynamics (wuxing), Ten Gods (shishen 十神), chart structure (geju 格局)
- **Less validated layer**: Many Shen Sha -- practitioners say they "add color to life" but "on their own would not be able to tell how successful a person would be"

### Lack of Modern Statistical Studies
- No peer-reviewed statistical studies specifically testing Shen Sha predictions were found
- Dean (2025): meta-analyses of ~300 empirical astrological studies found no reliable predictive power beyond chance
- BaZi described academically as "a fractured landscape, limited by tradition and a fundamental lack of empirical validation"

## 4. Shared Chart Problem (共盤問題)

### The Problem
People born within the same 2-hour shichen 時辰 share identical BaZi charts but have divergent life trajectories. This is empirically testable, not philosophical speculation.

### Traditional Responses
1. **Sub-divisions**: The shichen can be broken into smaller units, but these are "virtually impossible to measure and incorporate"
2. **Environmental factors**: Feng shui, family, education, free will
3. **Emperor and Beekeeper folklore**: Structural parallel, different magnitude
4. **Prenatal factors**: Twins may receive different Qi proportions in utero

### Statistical Analysis Status
- **No rigorous demographic or statistical study found in academic literature**
- Discussion remains anecdotal within practitioner community
- This is genuinely falsifiable and testable with birth registry data
- Some emerging projects (Lifelog Canvas) aim to create large-scale databases but none have published results

### Scale of the Problem
The approximately 518,400 distinct BaZi configurations (60 year x 12 month x 60 day x 12 hour, with constraints) serve a global Chinese-heritage population of billions. Even within a single country, thousands of people share each chart.

## 6. Corrections to Initial Assumptions

1. **Compiler**: Primary supervisor was Prince Yunlu 允祿, not Mei Juecheng alone. Mei was one of several co-compilers alongside He Guozong.

2. **Date**: Completed 1739, not 1741.

3. **"Big data" for SMTH**: Anachronistic but defensible as analogy. Recommend "proto-empirical encyclopedic synthesis."

4. **Wan Minying birth year**: 1521 (Kotyk) or 1523 (Baidu) -- sources disagree.

5. **SMTH date**: Completed c. 1578, not "1550s." The 1550 date corresponds to Wan's jinshi examination.
