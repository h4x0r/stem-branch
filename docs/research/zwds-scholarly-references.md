## 1. Historical Origins & Chen Tuan (陳摶)

### 1.1 Ho Peng Yoke — Ziwei Doushu and Astrology Origins

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Author | Ho Peng Yoke (何丙郁) |
| Title | 「紫微斗數」與星占學的淵源 |
| Publication | 《歷史月刊》(Historical Monthly), no. 68, pp. 50--56 |
| Year | 1993 |
| URL | Referenced in: https://www.ihp.sinica.edu.tw/~medicine/active/years/astro.PDF |

**Note:** The *only* known scholarly article specifically about Ziwei Doushu's astrological origins. Ho Peng Yoke (1926--2014) was Director of the Needham Research Institute, Cambridge. Published in a Taiwanese popular-academic magazine (not peer-reviewed). Brief (7 pages).

### 1.2 Ho Peng Yoke — Chinese Mathematical Astrology

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Author | Ho Peng Yoke (何丙郁) |
| Title | *Chinese Mathematical Astrology: Reaching Out to the Stars* |
| Publisher | London: RoutledgeCurzon (Needham Research Institute Series) |
| Year | 2003 |
| Pages | 201 pp. |
| ISBN | 978-0-415-29759-2 |
| URL | https://www.routledge.com/Chinese-Mathematical-Astrology-Reaching-Out-to-the-Stars/Yoke/p/book/9780415863100 |
| Review | *East Asian Science, Technology, and Medicine* 23(1), 2005, p. 134. https://brill.com/view/journals/east/23/1/article-p134_8.xml |

**Note:** Covers the three "cosmic board" (三式) systems: Taiyi (太乙), Qimen Dunjia (奇門遁甲), Liuren (六壬). These are the computational mantic systems used by the Chinese Astronomical Bureau from the 11th century. Does NOT specifically cover Ziwei Doushu, but provides essential context for Chinese mathematical astrology as a class of deterministic fate-computation algorithms.

### 1.3 Chen Tuan (陳摶) — Primary Historical Sources

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Primary source | 《宋史》(History of the Song Dynasty), "陳摶傳" (Biography of Chen Tuan) |
| Subject dates | 872--989 CE |
| Wikipedia | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chen_Tuan |

**Note:** Chen Tuan is traditionally credited with creating Ziwei Doushu through astronomical observation. He is also credited with the Wuji Tu (無極圖) and Xiantian Tu (先天圖) that influenced Song Neo-Confucianism (Zhou Dunyi, Shao Yong). The attribution of 《紫微斗數全書》 to Chen Tuan is *likely pseudepigraphical* -- the earliest datable editions are from the Ming dynasty (16th century).

## 3. The Computational Algorithm

### 3.1 Chinese Wikipedia -- 紫微斗數

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| URL | https://zh.wikipedia.org/zh-hant/紫微斗數 |

**Note:** Surprisingly comprehensive and well-sourced for algorithmic details:
- **命宮 determination:** 寅起正月, 順數至生月, 逆數生時
- **五行局:** Determined via 納音 of the Heavenly Stem/Earthly Branch at 命宮
- **紫微星 placement:** Formula using birth day divided by 五行局 number
- **十四主星:** Two series -- 紫微星系 (counterclockwise with gaps) and 天府星系 (clockwise with one gap)
- **四化:** Determined by birth year's Heavenly Stem

## 8. Important Caveats

1. **No dedicated monograph on Ziwei Doushu exists in any Western language.** The system remains under-studied in English-language academia compared to Yijing divination or BaZi (Four Pillars).

2. **Ho Peng Yoke's 1993 article in Historical Monthly** is the closest thing to a dedicated scholarly treatment of ZWDS origins. It is in Chinese, in a popular-academic magazine, and is brief (7 pages).

3. **The most relevant Chinese-language academic databases are:**
   - CNKI (中國知網): http://www.cnki.net/ -- search "紫微斗數"
   - National Digital Library of Theses and Dissertations in Taiwan: https://ndltd.ncl.edu.tw/ -- several master's theses exist
   - Airiti Library: https://www.airitilibrary.com/ -- Taiwanese academic journals

4. **The Chinese Wikipedia article on 紫微斗數** is surprisingly comprehensive and well-sourced for algorithmic details. It should be verified against primary texts (《紫微斗數全書》) but serves as a useful technical reference.

5. **Jeffrey Kotyk's work on Chinese horoscopy** provides the strongest scholarly bridge between ZWDS and Western astrological traditions, even though he does not focus on ZWDS specifically. His analysis of the twelve-house system in *Xingxue dacheng* (星學大成) is directly relevant to understanding ZWDS's twelve-palace structure.
