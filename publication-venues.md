# Publication Venue Research: "Why Your Chinese Metaphysics App Gives Wrong Results"

*Research conducted 2026-03-22*

Paper profile: 14pp, two-column, pgfplots charts. Covers VSOP87D ephemeris, solar term calculation, JS library cross-validation against sxwnl/JPL Horizons, four Chinese metaphysical systems, historical prime meridians, anthropological/history-of-science elements.

---

## 1. Preprint Servers (Immediate Dissemination)

### 1.1 arXiv

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Cost | Free |
| Peer review | None (moderation only) |
| Turnaround | 1-2 business days |
| License | Author chooses (CC-BY, CC-BY-SA, CC-BY-NC-SA, or arXiv non-exclusive) |

**Recommended categories (primary + cross-list):**

- **`astro-ph.IM`** (Instrumentation and Methods for Astrophysics) -- Primary. The VSOP87D ephemeris implementation, solar longitude computation, and cross-validation against JPL Horizons fit squarely here. Precedent: papers about astronomical calendar computation and ephemeris validation appear in astro-ph.IM.
- **`physics.hist-ph`** (History and Philosophy of Physics) -- Cross-list. The historical prime meridian analysis, Chinese calendar reform history, and anthropological framing fit. Precedent: Hayakawa et al. on Chinese historical sunspot records (arXiv:1705.xxxxx) cross-listed astro-ph.SR and physics.hist-ph.
- **`cs.SE`** (Software Engineering) -- Cross-list. The software validation methodology (cross-validating a JS library against reference implementations) has a software engineering angle. Precedent: DAME (arXiv:1010.4843) cross-listed astro-ph.IM and cs.SE.

**Verdict:** Excellent first step. Post here before or simultaneously with journal submission. The interdisciplinary cross-listing captures all audiences.

## 2. Peer-Reviewed Journals

### Tier 1: Best Fit (Interdisciplinary History of Astronomy)

#### 2.1 Journal of Astronomical History and Heritage (JAHH)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Publisher | University of Science and Technology of China |
| Open access | Yes, fully OA |
| APC | Page charges (amount not publicly listed; contact editors) |
| Peer review | Yes, refereed |
| Frequency | Quarterly (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec) |
| Impact | Scopus-indexed, IF ~0.94 |
| Editors | Wayne Orchiston & Yunli Shi |

**Fit assessment: EXCELLENT.** This journal publishes on "all aspects of astronomical history and heritage" including studies that "place the nature and evolution of astronomy in a social, historical, and cultural context." The paper's blend of ephemeris computation, Chinese calendar history, and prime meridian analysis is a natural fit. The co-editor Yunli Shi specializes in Chinese astronomy history. Precedent: the journal published on Jesuit astronomers and Chinese calendar reform (arXiv:2006.16766).

**Verdict:** Top recommendation. The editorial expertise in Chinese astronomical history is unmatched.

#### 2.2 Journal for the History of Astronomy (JHA)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Publisher | SAGE Publications |
| Open access | Hybrid (OA option available) |
| APC | SAGE OA fee applies if choosing OA |
| Peer review | Yes |
| Frequency | Quarterly |
| Impact | SSCI-indexed, IF ~0.3 |
| Submission format | Word, email attachment |
| Founded | 1970 |

**Fit assessment: VERY GOOD.** "The only journal devoted to the history of astronomy from earliest times to the present." Subject matter "extends to allied fields including the history of relevant branches of mathematics and physics, and the use of historical records in the service of astronomy." The computational validation angle may need to be de-emphasized in favor of the historical narrative.

**Verdict:** Strong alternative to JAHH. More established but lower IF. The paper may need slight reframing to emphasize historical over computational aspects.

#### 2.3 Culture and Cosmos

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Publisher | Sophia Centre Press / University of Wales Trinity Saint David |
| Open access | Yes, fully OA (since 2017) |
| APC | None (zero fees) |
| Peer review | Yes |
| Frequency | 2 issues/year |
| Scope | History of astrology and cultural astronomy |

**Fit assessment: GOOD.** The "only peer-reviewed journal to focus on the history of astrology and cultural aspects of the history of astronomy." The metaphysical systems (BaZi, Qimen Dunjia, Zi Wei Dou Shu, Da Liu Ren) are astrological/divinatory in nature, making this a natural home for the cultural dimension. However, the computational astronomy core may be less central to their typical readership.

**Verdict:** Good option if the paper emphasizes the cultural/astrological dimensions. The zero-fee OA model is attractive.

#### 2.4 Journal of Astronomy in Culture (JAC)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Publisher | eScholarship (University of California) |
| Open access | Yes |
| Peer review | Double-anonymous |
| Founded | 2016 |
| Scope | Academic investigations into astronomies and their cultural contexts |

**Fit assessment: GOOD.** Covers "the operation of astronomies and the cultures, societies and ideologies that supported them" from earliest times to present. Methodological approaches from anthropology, archaeology, archaeoastronomy welcome.

**Verdict:** Viable alternative, especially if emphasizing the cultural embeddedness of Chinese astronomical computation.

### Tier 2: History of Science (Broader)

#### 2.5 East Asian Science, Technology, and Medicine (EASTM)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Publisher | Brill (for ISHEASTM) |
| Open access | Hybrid |
| APC | Brill's standard OA fee if OA chosen |
| Peer review | Yes |
| Scope | History of East Asian science, technology, medicine |
| Page limits | None (accepts lengthy, empirically rich articles) |
| Note | Formerly titled *Chinese Science* (1975-1998) |

**Fit assessment: VERY GOOD.** This journal (the successor to *Chinese Science*) is dedicated to "the study of traditional, modern, and contemporary East Asian science, technology, and medicine." The no-page-limit policy is unusual and valuable for a 14-page paper with charts. The paper's use of Chinese primary source concepts (solar terms, metaphysical systems) and historical analysis fits the journal's mission perfectly.

**Verdict:** Strong recommendation. The journal's identity as the successor to *Chinese Science* and its explicit focus on East Asian scientific traditions make it ideal. The computational validation angle adds a modern methodological dimension they would likely welcome.

## 4. Recommended Publication Strategy

### Phase 1: Immediate (Week 1)
1. **Post to arXiv** under `astro-ph.IM` (primary), cross-listed with `physics.hist-ph` and `cs.SE`
2. **Archive on Zenodo** with DOI for the library source code and validation datasets

### Phase 2: Journal Submission (Weeks 2-4)
**Primary target:** Submit to one of these (in order of recommendation):

1. **JAHH** -- Best overall fit. Chinese astronomy expertise on editorial board. Fully OA. Moderate page charges.
2. **EASTM (Brill)** -- Excellent fit for historical East Asian science. No page limits. Successor to *Chinese Science*.
3. **Journal for the History of Astronomy** -- Established, SAGE-published, broader audience.
4. **Culture and Cosmos** -- Zero-fee OA, good cultural astronomy fit.
5. **Journal of Cultural Analytics** -- Zero-fee OA, fast turnaround, computational culture angle.

### Phase 3: Conference Presentation (Months 2-6)
1. **DH2026** (Daejeon, South Korea) -- Submit by Sep 30, 2025. East Asian location is ideal.
2. **ADASS XXXVI** (Perth, 2026) -- Submit a condensed computational-methods version.

### Phase 4: Optional
- Separate **JOSS** submission for the stem-branch library itself
- **SSRN** posting for social science / humanities visibility
